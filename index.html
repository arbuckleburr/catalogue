Curated directory of external collections. These institutions have valuable public-domain holdings but don't expose APIs we can search programmatically. Each card links directly to the source — browse them on their own sites, where their full context and metadata are preserved. All listed collections are in the public domain or marked "no known copyright restrictions"; commercial use is permitted, no attribution required.
Mugshots & Criminal Records
4 collections
NSW Police Forensic Photography Archive
Justice & Police Museum, Sydney · 1910–1964
~130,000 glass-plate and cellulose negatives from New South Wales police. The "Special Photographs" subset (~2,500 from 1910–1930) is among the most remarkable mugshot collections in existence — subjects posed themselves, often still animated by the dramas of their arrest.
Open ↗
Leavenworth Federal Penitentiary Inmate Case Files
NARA Kansas City · 1895–1957
Historical federal prison case files, most including mugshots, accessible through NARA's online catalog. Browseable by surname index. Genuinely vast — includes figures from Robert Stroud (the Birdman of Alcatraz) to thousands of lesser-known prisoners.
Open ↗
PICRYL Mugshots Collection
Aggregated PD media · global
Curated mugshot collection pulled from NARA, state archives, and international PD sources. Easier to browse thematically than digging through NARA's catalog directly.
Open ↗
Public Record Office Victoria
Victoria, Australia
Historical Victorian (Australian state) prison registers with mugshots, available online. Particularly strong in late-19th and early-20th-century material. Public domain.

Historical & Specialty Photography
4 collections
Getty Open Content Program
J. Paul Getty Museum & Research Institute · CC0
160,000+ high-resolution images, all CC0. Particularly strong in early photography (calotypes, ambrotypes, daguerreotypes, gelatin silver, cyanotypes) and the Foto Arte Minore archive of Italian art and architecture.
Open ↗
George Eastman Museum
Rochester, NY · photography archive
The world's largest photography archive, especially strong in early photography. Substantial open material; access is browse-based via their online collection.
Open ↗
British Library on Flickr Commons
"Mechanical Curator" collection · 1 million+ images
One million+ images extracted from 17th–19th century books: illustrations, maps, photographs, advertisements, ornamental letters and borders. All marked "no known copyright restrictions."
Open ↗
Library of Congress "Free to Use" Sets
curated thematic groupings · LoC
Curated thematic collections from LoC's holdings, specifically pre-cleared as PD or rights-cleared. A faster entry point than searching LoC's full catalog if you want a starting place by subject.

Federal Agencies
4 collections
FBI Vault
Federal Bureau of Investigation · FOIA reading room
The FBI's public FOIA reading room. Historical case files, surveillance photography from declassified investigations, vintage wanted posters (Dillinger, Bonnie & Clyde, etc.). Browseable, not API-searchable.
Open ↗
US Marshals Service Historical Photographs
USMS · history page
Frontier-era photography and historical Marshals operations. Smaller collection than FBI/NARA but distinct material — particularly strong on 19th-century American West.
Open ↗
EPA DOCUMERICA
Environmental Protection Agency · 1971–1977
A landmark social-documentary photography project where the EPA hired photographers to document environmental conditions and everyday life across America in the early 1970s. Held at NARA, also browseable as a Flickr set.
Open ↗
National Park Service Historic Photo Collection
NPS · early 1900s onward
Landscape and historical photography of the American national parks system since its founding. Strong on monumental landscape — Yosemite, Yellowstone, the Grand Canyon — plus historical documentation of park development.
Open ↗
Curated Public-Domain Aggregators
6 collections
The Public Domain Review
curated journal · themed collections
Curated journal of out-of-copyright works with extraordinary themed collections. Each piece checked for PD status. The "show me something I'd never have found" resource — strong on strange, beautiful, and historically unusual material.
Open ↗
Public Domain Image Archive
curated PD image collection
Hand-picked PD collection from The Public Domain Review, "a living database with new images added every week." Easier to browse than Wikimedia Commons; more art-friendly than NARA.
Open ↗
PICRYL (general)
PD media search engine · global
Broad PD media search engine pulling from NARA, NASA, Library of Congress, and many others. Functionally similar to DPLA but with different coverage and a more visual, browse-oriented interface.
Open ↗
Flickr Commons
flickr.com/commons · participating institutions
The Flickr Commons central page listing all participating institutions worldwide. We can't API into Flickr for commercial use, but browsing it directly gives you links to the underlying institutions and their material.
Open ↗
OPenn (Penn Libraries)
University of Pennsylvania · open manuscripts
Penn's open manuscript archive. Especially strong in medieval manuscripts (Ms. Codex collections) and rare books. Browse by collection rather than searching — but the material is extraordinary and entirely PD/CC0.
Open ↗
The Walters Art Museum
Baltimore · medieval & Islamic manuscripts
Strong medieval and Islamic manuscript collection. Mixed CC0 and CC-BY-SA licensing — use with awareness, browse to pick CC0-tagged items if attribution-tracking matters.

API keys

Keys live in this browser's localStorage — they aren't sent anywhere except to the source they belong to, and they aren't part of the app file. If you ever change devices or clear browser data, you'll re-enter them. Don't enter keys on a shared computer.
National Archives (NARA) not set
Free, read-only. Request by email to Catalog_API@nara.gov — they reply within a day or two with your key.
Digital Public Library of America (DPLA) not set
Free, instant. From a terminal run:
curl -XPOST https://api.dp.la/v2/api_key/your_email@example.com
The 32-character key arrives by email.
Internet Archive needs no key.
